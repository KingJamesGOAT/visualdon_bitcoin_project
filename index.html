1. The Cypherpunk Network

In 2009, the Bitcoin network was highly decentralized. It was maintained by thousands of individual "cypherpunks" and early computing enthusiasts.

Wealth was spread incredibly thin. Hover over the nodes in the visualizer to see the tiny fragment of the total supply each individual held during the genesis era.

2. Institutional Takeover

As the asset matured and liquidity deepened, major financial players entered the arena. Publicly traded companies and dedicated investment trusts began sweeping up massive portions of the available supply.

The network fragments merged. Notice how the vast ocean of retail users is overshadowed by massive corporate entities.

3. Global State Accumulation

The arms race eventually escalated to the sovereign level. Entire nation-states began integrating the asset into their central bank reserves.

Click on the designated countries on the map to view the precise absolute holdings of various national treasuries gathered via seizures and strategic acquisitions.

4. The Historical Wealth Gap

Viewing this phenomenon historically reveals a distinct trend. Over the past decade, the relative percentage of the supply held by early retail participants has plummeted.

Simultaneously, institutional custody solutions ballooned. Track your cursor across the timeline to view exact yearly proportions.

5. The Modern Distribution

Today, the vision of a purely decentralized peer-to-peer cash network has transitioned into a highly structured institutional settlement layer.

This exact current market snapshot breaks down ownership categories. Hover over the bars to isolate each demographic and expose the true magnitude of accumulation.
