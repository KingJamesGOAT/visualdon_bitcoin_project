The Cypherpunk Era

In the beginning, Bitcoin was a highly decentralized network. Thousands of independent retail users, cypherpunks, and early adopters collectively ran the network and mined blocks from their personal computers.

The network value was distributed broadly among many tiny nodes, each acting as an equal peer in the system.

Institutional Entry

As the asset matured, large financial institutions, public companies, and dedicated mining pools entered the space.

Capital began to consolidate. The fragmented retail hashpower and holdings were dwarfed by massive corporate entities, centralizing control and influence into a few major players.

Global Accumulation

The accumulation didn't stop at the corporate level. Soon, sovereign nation states began aggressively acquiring the asset to bolster their reserves or for strategic geopolitical positioning.

Huge swathes of the available supply are now geographically concentrated in select regulatory jurisdictions and state treasuries.

The Modern Reality

Today's Bitcoin market looks vastly different from its origins. While the protocol remains open, the actual distribution of wealth and infrastructure has become definitively centralized.

Institutional and concentrated capital vastly outweighs the original cypherpunk retail base in both volume and market influence.
